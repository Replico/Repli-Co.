About

My name is Dominick LaCasse and this project is a culmination of something I have wondered about for my whole life: the truth. I have come to realize in my old age that truth will always be somewhat subjective, considering we are on a floating rock hurdling through the unknown. But, from what I have noticed, people obtain their believes from a few parameters in their lives: nature, nurture, and experience outside of familial custody. Personally, I feel lucky to not have had to deal with parents who felt so inclined to indoctrinate me with their beliefs. While I know I'm not bias-free, I do think that I was allowed to have a perspective that allowed me to find out what I wanted to know through a personal search for the truth. I found that I can ask people what they think about a certain subject, or even look it up on the internet. But the skills that I am most proud of obtaining are both the skills of skeptacism and trust. These skills sounds contradictory, but they go hand in hand. When coming from a point of bias or lack of knowledge, skeptacism is in full control, rightfully so. But an important skill that I learned to pair with skepticism is trust. And the ability to develop trust in the light of skeptacism is important for not only forming your own opinion, but for governing a body of vastly different people. I whole-heartedly believe that there is a systematic objectiveness nested inside of subjectivism. And that is my entire goal here. Get people to realize their biases, or at least have the quantification and qualification of their believes objectively plastered in front of their face. So in your face, skepticists without trust. Sincererly though, please reach out to me if you have any comments on objectivism, subjectivism, or the truth in general. I am sure we will never agree down to the most base level on everything, but I dutifully hope that this project can at least show where we disagree about foundational human concepts and why.
