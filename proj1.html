Background

This project is an attempt to systematically and mathematically prove one's beliefs based on their answers to simple value questions. My personal philospohy is that one's personal philospohy on life and the foundation of truth in which they live their lives can be determined by a series of yes/no questions from a high-level question like "Do you believe in God?". My goal is to categorize people into parties that aren't somewhat subjective and hyporcritical/evil like Democrats or Republicans. I believe that there are parties that each one of us belongs to that tailor our values much better than what the Big 2 can do. If people realize that political and human values are quite intersectional and not black-and-white, then much more progress will be made towards solving problems and setting goals rather than succumbing to the complacency that modern politics offers the average person, like me.

Methodology

The Methodology for this project may seem simple, but has a lot of hidden complexities. The goal to categorize people starting with a high-level question is ambitious but it needs to be tried. The main problem I have run into while constructing this categorization is balancing categorizing people into groups of people with similar values versus having some groups be too niche, or have groups that have values based on hate or terror. There is no room for a categorization of people based on hate or terror. Therefore, I have come to the conclusion that 3 is the number of questions past the high-level question that I will ask to categorize people. This number is arbitrary and quite subject to change. However I think that combining multiple high-level questions with branches that equal 23= 8 questions is the perfect amount of complexity to accurately group people in a way that is much more objective and efficient than traditional party classifications.
